Varanasi Trekking Experiences

Introduction

Varanasi, one of the oldest cities in the world, offers a unique trekking experience that blends spirituality, history, and cultural exploration.

While Varanasi is not traditionally known for trekking, the city's intricate alleys, the ghats along the Ganges, and nearby Sarnath make for interesting urban treks.

Trek Highlights:

  • Ghats of Varanasi: Trek along the ghats (riverbanks) of the Ganges, from Assi Ghat to Dashashwamedh Ghat, observing the spiritual rituals and the life of the city.
  • Temples and Shrines: Explore ancient temples such as Kashi Vishwanath Temple, Durga Temple, and the Sankat Mochan Hanuman Temple.
  • Nearby Villages: Day treks to nearby villages for a glimpse of rural life around Varanasi.
  • Sarnath Trek: Visit Sarnath, the place where Lord Buddha delivered his first sermon, just 10 km from Varanasi.

Cultural Exploration:

  • Experience the mystical Ganga Aarti at Dashashwamedh Ghat and explore the rich heritage of this sacred city.
  • Wander through narrow alleys filled with local markets, traditional houses, and old buildings.

Vanarashi Tour Plan

Tour Duration: 4 Days

Day-by-Day Trekking Itinerary


Day 1: Arrival and Local Exploration

Morning: Arrive in Varanasi and check into your hotel. After settling in, enjoy breakfast at the hotel or a local café.

Mid-Morning: Visit the Kashi Vishwanath Temple, one of the most revered temples dedicated to Lord Shiva. Take time to appreciate the intricate architecture and spiritual ambiance.

Lunch: Enjoy lunch at a local restaurant offering traditional Varanasi cuisine, such as Chaat or Puri Sabzi.

Afternoon: Stroll through the narrow alleys of Varanasi, visiting local markets. Don’t miss Dashashwamedh Ghat, where you can witness the vibrant atmosphere along the Ganges.

Evening: Attend the mesmerizing Ganga Aarti at Dashashwamedh Ghat. Experience the spiritual rituals and the beauty of the evening prayer performed by priests, illuminated by lamps.


Day 2: Ghats and Spiritual Trek

Morning: After breakfast, prepare for a trek along the ghats. Start from Assi Ghat and make your way to Manikarnika Ghat, the main cremation ghat. Experience the spiritual essence of the city as you walk along the riverbank.

Mid-Morning: Stop at various temples along the way, including Sankat Mochan Hanuman Temple and Durga Temple. Take in the sights, sounds, and scents of the local environment.

Lunch: Enjoy lunch at a restaurant near Manikarnika Ghat, savoring local delicacies.

Afternoon: Continue your trek to Raja Ghat and explore the surrounding areas. Visit small shops and interact with local artisans.

Evening: Head back to your hotel for a refreshing shower. Optional: Attend a local cultural show or classical music performance in the evening.


Day 3: Trek to Sarnath

Morning: After breakfast, prepare for a day trek to Sarnath, located about 10 km from Varanasi. You can choose to hire a local guide to enhance your experience.

Mid-Morning: Arrive in Sarnath and visit the Dhamek Stupa, where Buddha gave his first sermon. Explore the ruins and learn about the history and significance of this place.

Lunch: Enjoy lunch at a nearby café or local eatery in Sarnath, trying some authentic Buddhist vegetarian dishes.

Afternoon: Visit the Archaeological Museum in Sarnath, which houses numerous artifacts related to Buddhism. Spend time exploring the serene gardens and other monuments in the area.

Evening: Return to Varanasi, freshen up, and enjoy a leisurely dinner at a restaurant overlooking the Ganges.


Day 4: Departure or Optional Rural Trek

Morning: After breakfast, check out of the hotel. Depending on your travel plans, you can either head to the airport or railway station.

Optional Activity: If you have extra time, consider a rural trek to nearby villages like Chunar or Ramnagar. Experience local village life, visit traditional homes, and enjoy the scenic landscapes.

Lunch: Enjoy a picnic lunch in the countryside before heading back to Varanasi.

Afternoon: Wrap up your visit with some last-minute shopping for souvenirs like Banarasi sarees, handicrafts, and brassware.

Evening: Depart from Varanasi with cherished memories of your trek and exploration of this spiritually enriching city.

Additional Information

Cultural Insights
  • Historical Significance: Varanasi, also known as Kashi, is one of the oldest inhabited cities in the world. It is considered the spiritual capital of India, with a history dating back thousands of years. The city is a major cultural hub for Hinduism, Buddhism, and Jainism, attracting pilgrims from all over the country and beyond.
  • Festivals: If your visit coincides with festivals like Dev Deepawali (celebrated in November) or Holi (March), be sure to participate in the vibrant celebrations. These festivals showcase the rich traditions and communal spirit of Varanasi, featuring music, dance, and elaborate decorations.
  • Local Arts and Crafts: Varanasi is famous for its Banarasi silk sarees, renowned for their intricate designs and quality. Visiting local weavers can provide insight into this traditional craft. The city is also known for brassware and wooden toys. Engaging with local artisans can be a unique experience.
Local Cuisine Recommendations
  • Street Food: Try local delicacies like Kachori (fried bread filled with lentils) and Chaat (a savory snack made with potatoes, chickpeas, and spices). Don’t miss the famous Banarasi Paan, a betel leaf preparation that is a popular post-meal treat.
  • Restaurants:
    • Kashi Chaat Bhandar: Known for its delicious street food.
    • BrijRama Palace: A historic hotel with a restaurant offering a beautiful view of the Ganges and a menu featuring local cuisine.
  • Sweet Treats: Try local sweets like Malaiyo (a frothy milk dessert) and Rabri (a sweet, condensed milk dish). These are often found in local sweet shops.
Essential Tips for Travelers
  • Dress Modestly: Varanasi is a sacred city, so it’s advisable to dress modestly, especially when visiting temples and religious sites.
  • Respect Local Customs: While exploring the ghats and temples, be mindful of local customs and traditions. For instance, removing shoes before entering temples is customary.
  • Stay Hydrated: Varanasi can get hot, especially in summer. Carry a water bottle and stay hydrated throughout your trek.
  • Local Guides: Hiring a local guide can enhance your experience by providing insights into the city’s rich history, culture, and traditions.
  • Safety and Health: Be cautious about food hygiene when trying street food. Drink bottled or filtered water to avoid waterborne illnesses.
  • Transportation: Use cycle rickshaws or auto-rickshaws for short distances. Walking is a great way to explore the narrow lanes and ghats.
  • Photography: While photography is allowed at many places, always ask for permission before taking pictures of people, especially in rural areas.

Explore Vanarshi

Location

Varanasi, also known as Kashi, is situated in the northern Indian state of Uttar Pradesh. It lies on the banks of the holy Ganges River and is about 800 km (497 miles) from the national capital, New Delhi. The city is known for its ghats, temples, and rich cultural heritage.

Key Locations in Varanasi
1. Dashashwamedh Ghat

This is the most famous ghat in Varanasi, known for the Ganga Aarti (a Hindu ritual of worship) held every evening. It attracts thousands of visitors and offers a mesmerizing view of the Ganges.

2. Kashi Vishwanath Temple

One of the twelve Jyotirlingas, this temple is dedicated to Lord Shiva and is a significant pilgrimage site for Hindus. The temple's architecture is magnificent, and it draws devotees from all over India.

3. Manikarnika Ghat

Considered the main cremation ghat in Varanasi, Manikarnika Ghat holds immense spiritual significance. It's a place where many Hindus choose to be cremated to attain moksha (liberation).

4. Sarnath

Located about 10 km (6 miles) from Varanasi, Sarnath is where Gautama Buddha gave his first sermon after attaining enlightenment. It features several important Buddhist sites, including the Dhamek Stupa and the Sarnath Archaeological Museum.

5. Assi Ghat

This ghat is famous for its serene atmosphere and beautiful sunrise views. It's an excellent spot for yoga and meditation and offers boat rides along the Ganges.

Travel Tips for Visiting Varanasi
  • Best Time to Visit: The ideal time to visit Varanasi is from October to March when the weather is pleasant.
  • Getting Around: Use cycle rickshaws, auto-rickshaws, or hire a local guide for navigating the narrow lanes of the city.
  • Language: Hindi is the primary language spoken, but English is widely understood in tourist areas.
  • Local Transport: Varanasi has a railway station and is well connected by road. You can also fly into Lal Bahadur Shastri International Airport.
  • Safety: Varanasi is generally safe for tourists, but it's always wise to stay cautious, especially in crowded places.
